# Why Mindlura Gets More Accurate Over Time

Some users ask after their first month: *"Why doesn't my Psychology Score feel accurate yet?"* or *"Why are the recommendations still too general?"*

The answer is simple: **one month and 10 trades isn't enough.**

This isn't a limitation — it's the nature of trading psychology itself.

---

## Trading Psychology Is Pattern-Based, Not Moment-Based

Trading mistakes aren't random. A trader who closes positions early out of fear does it repeatedly — not just once. A trader who revenge trades after a loss shows that pattern under specific conditions.

Mindlura is built to detect these **recurring patterns**. And by definition, a pattern requires repetition.

With 10 trades, you might have revenge traded once — or maybe you didn't. Neither conclusion is statistically meaningful. But with 200 trades, if you entered with a larger lot size 35 times after consecutive losses, that's a measurable, actionable pattern.

---

## Less Data = Incomplete Analysis

Imagine you want to know whether you trade better during the London or New York session. With 5 trades in each, you can't draw any conclusion. With 150 trades in each, the answer becomes clear.

The same principle applies to everything Mindlura analyzes:

| Data Volume | What You See |
|-------------|-------------|
| 10–50 trades | Basic stats, no clear patterns |
| 50–200 trades | Early patterns in session performance and R:R |
| 200+ trades | Deep psychology: triggers, biases, behavior under pressure |

---

## Why Journaling Matters

Your Psychology Score isn't built from MT5 data alone. When you log how you felt before or after a trade, Mindlura can find the connection between your **mental state** and your **trade outcome**.

For example:
- Did you lose more when you felt "confident"?
- Did trades you took with "hesitation" actually have better R:R?
- Does your entry quality drop after 3 consecutive losses?

These questions can't be answered without a journal.

## How Long Does It Take?

A realistic timeline:

**1–3 months (50–150 trades)**
An initial picture of your trading style, broad strengths and weaknesses

**3–6 months (150–300 trades)**
Clear behavioral patterns, a Psychology Score you can rely on

**6+ months (300+ trades)**
Deep behavioral analysis, pattern prediction, comparison across time periods
